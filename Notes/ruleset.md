# PIPELINE RESTORATION SOLITAIRE  

## FULL RULESET DOCUMENT

---

# 1. GAME OVERVIEW

Pipeline Restoration Solitaire is a hybrid patience game combining FreeCell‑style sequencing with layered obstacle clearing.  
The player restores a broken water pipeline by organizing Tools, clearing Challenges with Solutions, and building Impact chains.

The game ends when the player uncovers the Well Card and applies the final Solution.

---

# 2. CARD TYPES

## Tools (T)

- Prepare or weaken Challenges.
- Can be sequenced together.
- Often required before Solutions can be applied.

## Challenges (C)

- Block progress in tableau columns.
- Some freeze columns or spread penalties.
- Some cannot be moved or stored.

## Solutions (S)

- Clear specific Challenges.
- Reveal hidden or locked layers.
- Advance the Solutions Foundation.

## Impact Cards (I)

- Provide score multipliers.
- Reveal hidden cards.
- Grant free moves or prevent penalties.
- Can be played at any time.

---

# 3. GAME LAYOUT

## Tableau

- 8 columns of layered cards.
- Some cards are hidden or locked behind Challenges.
- Depth varies to create a “digging” effect.

## Water Tanks (Free Cells)

- 4–6 temporary storage slots.
- Hold cards while reorganizing sequences.
- Some card types cannot be stored.

## Foundations

Four piles must be completed:

1. Tools  
2. Solutions  
3. Challenges Cleared  
4. Impact Cards  

## Well Card

- Buried at the bottom of the deepest column.
- Must be uncovered and resolved with the final Solution to win.

---

# 4. SETUP

1. Shuffle the 52‑card deck.  
2. Deal cards into 8 tableau columns with varying depths.  
3. Place 4–6 Water Tanks above the tableau.  
4. Place 4 empty Foundations to the right.  
5. Place the Well Card at the bottom of the deepest column.

---

# 5. TURN STRUCTURE

On any turn, the player may:

- Move a card to another column (if sequence is valid).  
- Move a card to a Water Tank (if allowed).  
- Move a card to a Foundation (if valid).  
- Apply a Solution to a Challenge.  
- Use Tools to prepare Challenges.  
- Reveal hidden cards when unlocked.  
- Use Boosters (if available).

Only the top card of each column may be moved unless unlocked.

---

# 6. SEQUENCING RULES

## Valid Sequences

- Tool → Tool  
- Tool → Solution  
- Challenge → Solution  
- Tool → Challenge → Solution  
- Solution → Impact  
- Impact → Impact  

## Invalid Sequences

- Challenge → Challenge  
- Impact → Challenge  
- Solution → Tool  
- Impact → Tool  
- Solution → Challenge (wrong type)  

## Movement Rules

- Cards may move between columns only if they form a valid sequence.
- Cards may move to Water Tanks if allowed by type.
- Some Challenges cannot be moved or stored.
- Multi‑card moves may be allowed depending on ruleset.

---

# 9. IMPACT CARD EFFECTS

Impact Cards trigger bonuses when played or when specific events occur:

- Score multipliers  
- Free moves  
- Reveal hidden cards  
- Prevent next penalty  
- Unlock Well Card (specific Impact)  
- Bonus multipliers for chains  

Impact Cards may be stacked for cumulative effects.

---

# 10. LAYER‑CLEARING RULES

Cards may be:

- Face‑down  
- Locked behind Challenges  
- Buried under other cards  

To reveal them:

- Clear the Challenge above  
- Apply the correct Solution  
- Use Tools to weaken obstacles  
- Play Impact Cards that reveal layers  

---

# 11. TWIST MECHANICS

## Contamination Spread

Locks 2 random columns until solved.

## Heatwave

Temporarily reduces available moves.

## Water Theft

Steals an Impact Card unless countered immediately.

## Blocked Well

Prevents access to the final Well Card.

## Storage Limits

- Water Tanks are limited.
- Some cards cannot be stored.
- Some Solutions weaken if stored too long.

---

# 12. BOOSTERS (OPTIONAL RULES)

- Reveal Hidden Card  
- Minor Challenge Clear  
- Swap Two Cards  
- Freeze Penalty Timer  
- Temporary Extra Water Tank  

Boosters may be earned through:

- Clearing Challenges  
- Completing sequences  
- Impact bonuses  
- Progress milestones  

---

# 13. WIN CONDITIONS

The player wins when all of the following are true:

1. All Challenges are cleared.  
2. All four Foundations are complete.  
3. The Well Card is uncovered.  
4. The final Solution is placed on the Well Card.  

This triggers the win celebration and transitions to the Impact Screen.

---

# 14. FAIL CONDITIONS

The player loses when:

- No valid moves remain.  
- All Water Tanks are full and no sequences can be formed.  
- A spreading Challenge (e.g., Contamination) locks too many columns.
